About

I am a UX Leader with a decade of experience in high-density systems, but I am also an ISSA-Certified Personal Trainer and Run Coach. This dual perspective allows me to bridge the gap between physiological data and digital experience. Whether I am architecting a defense system for the DoD or a performance portal for athletes, my goal is the same: reducing friction to unlock human potential.

My journey into the digital world began at the Harvard Extension School in Cambridge, Massachusetts, where I took my first course in Online Development. There, I learned to code HTML and CSS from the ground up using nothing more than Notepad. Since then, I've built a diverse career across the digital landscape, contributing to design studios, large and small for-profit organizations, the healthcare sector, marketing agencies, start-ups, online education platforms, and most recently, the Department of Defense, where I served as a civilian member of the U.S. Air Force.

In my current work, I've expanded my focus beyond individual design execution to leading and shaping design practices at scale. As the lead of the Design System team, I've guided designers in creating scalable, consistent, and accessible components that empower entire product teams to work more efficiently. I'm also developing a comprehensive resource for managing design teams, establishing a library of process and research templates, frameworks for building design strategies, and clear roadmaps that align design vision with business objectives.

Recently, I've been exploring the integration of AI into the design process, leveraging it to accelerate research synthesis, inspire creative exploration, and streamline repetitive production tasks, freeing up teams to focus on higher-level problem solving. This combination of leadership, process development, and emerging technology exploration is helping me create not only great products, but great environments for designers to thrive.

Creatively, my process always begins with research. I validate personas, map user journeys, analyze competitors, and plan timelines and roadmaps. Gathering requirements and user stories from both stakeholders and users ensures every design starts with clarity and purpose. From there, I explore multiple design directions before refining the strongest concepts into wireframes.

Once wireframes are tested, I move into the detailed design phase, creating UI kits when none exist to streamline developer collaboration. I believe in continuous iteration, using post-launch feedback to evolve the experience so it always meets user needs.

Outside of work, I draw energy and focus from endurance sports. I compete in triathlons and am actively involved in local running and fitness communities, with races taking me to incredible places like St. Croix, the Dominican Republic, and Puerto Rico.
